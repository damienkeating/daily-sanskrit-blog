## Simple Sanskrit notes for learners — Theme: Royal Governance
- राजा (rājā) — king, ruler; lit. "one who shines or leads". king / 国王
- मन्त्री (mantrī) — minister, counselor; lit. "one who gives advice". minister / 大臣
- राज्यम् (rājyam) — kingdom, realm; lit. "that which is ruled". kingdom / 王国

## English
- On June 6, 913, eight-year-old Constantine VII became the nominal ruler of the Byzantine Empire.
- His uncle Alexander appointed a seven-man regency council headed by Patriarch Nicholas Mystikos to govern on the boy's behalf.
- This event illustrates how child monarchs often served as figureheads while real political power rested with appointed councils.

## Chinese
- 913年6月6日，八岁的君士坦丁七世成为拜占庭帝国的名义统治者。
- 他的叔父亚历山大在临终前任命了一个七人摄政会议，由牧首尼古拉斯·米斯蒂科斯领导，代表幼主执政。
- 这一事件说明，幼主君主常常只是傀儡，而真正的政治权力掌握在任命的会议手中。
